The Studio

What makes our classes different?

Arts beacon is Jena Dost’s working artist studio. Students will get to see my work in progress and preparations for exhibitions. Our classes are offered by the semester and there will be a student exhibition at the end of each semester. My teaching philosophy is to develop creative thinking and problem solving and flexibility and resilience and developing a rich visual aesthetic. Classes will be divided by age and will be offered age appropriate materials and tools. I have a variety of materials available to my students like clay, acrylic and watercolor painting, acrylic painting, fabric, woodworking tools and supplies, plaster, collage, mixed media sculptures and paintings. All of my students will have access to the studio tools, supplies and materials so they may gravitate to the medium that works best for their personal projects and goals. Students will be encouraged to draw from observation to learn how to see and develop a strong visual vocabulary and at the same time take a process based approach with materials to expand the possibilities of their art making.

Kids Drawing and Painting

Designed for 8-12 year-olds, this is a drawing and painting class with lots of experimenting, exploration and movement. We will focus on process-based art with an emphasis in exploring materials and developing a strong visual aesthetic by experimenting with color, line, texture, and 2-D design. Students will develop confidence as artists and will learn to celebrate their artistic differences. Dress to get messy! Students will use the studios paints, brushes, paper etc.

Mondays, 3:15-4:45
Tuition: $220 (includes materials fee)

Teen Creative Class

This is an all media class. Open to teens ages 13-18. This is a great class for tinkering, creative teens who love to make stuff, love to draw, paint, sculpt, and sew or are constantly making comics, board games or animations! I will give them time, encouragement and direction to create what they invision. Each class will end with a critique time for suggestions on how to think more deeply about their project. I will help students clarify their goals and direction and help guide them to making high quality professional art.

Wednesday: 5:30-7:00
Tuition: $250 (includes materials fee)

About Jena

Jena Dost is a working artist with 15 years of teaching experience. She holds a BFA from the Atlanta College of Art (Now SCAD) and an MFA from Queens College CUNY with majors in Sculpture and Drawing. She also completed the 2 year ceramics assistant program at Callanwolde Arts Center. She was an associate professor Brenau University and Oglethorpe University holding classes in Sculpture, Drawing, Painting, 3-D Design and Art Appreciation . She has taught classes, workshops and camps for preschool aged children through adult students at Callanwolde, Eyedrum, The Friends School, The Ormewood School and Grant Park Co-op.

See some of Jena's work at lynoredost.com.
